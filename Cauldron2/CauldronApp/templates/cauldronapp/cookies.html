What are cookies?

Cookies are small data files downloaded into the User’s device when he accesses the Website. Cookies allow information to be stored and retrieved about the browsing habits of a User or his equipment and, depending on the information contained and the way in which the equipment is used, cookies may be utilised in order to recognise the User.

Own cookies

They allow the Website to track your movement from page to page to ensure that you are not required to provide the same information as you have previously provided. Cookies allow you to proceed through the Website quickly and easily without having to authenticate or reprocess each new area you visit. We may use cookies to analyze user behavior, such as which pages have been visited during the session, to enhance and customize your overall experience, for example, allowing us to determine and display more relevant content.

There are two types of cookies:

  • Persistent cookies: remain on your computer and remain valid until you delete them or on their expiration date.
  • Session cookies: are deleted when you close your web browser.

Cookies may be used for technical reasons of the Website, for analysis and to connect to third party Websites or advertising.
